# OECS Post-Secondary SDG Questionnaire — Plain English Summary


## 1. What is this document, in one sentence?

It is a **survey form (questionnaire)** that colleges and universities in the Eastern Caribbean fill out once a year, so their government's Ministry of Education can collect education statistics and report progress on global education goals to the United Nations.

- **OECS** = Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States (a group of small Caribbean island countries — e.g. Grenada, St. Lucia, Dominica, Antigua).
- **Post-Secondary / Tertiary** = education *after* high school (colleges, universities, vocational training).
- **SKIP Component 1.1** = the name of the project/programme this form belongs to.
- It covers the **2025–2026 academic year**, and all numbers should describe the situation **as of 15th October 2025** (one fixed "snapshot" date).

**SDG stands for Sustainable Development Goals** — a set of **17 global goals adopted by the United Nations in 2015**, to be achieved by **2030**. They cover things like ending poverty, climate action, and education. This form deals only with **SDG 4 — "Quality Education,"** and specifically its sub-targets (codes like `4.3.2` or `4.c.1`). One of those, **Target 4.c**, is about increasing the supply of qualified teachers through training and recruitment.

The big idea: every table in the workbook is **tagged with an "SDG" code** that tells you which global goal that data feeds. Colours in the real spreadsheet match a colour key on the front sheet.

## 2. The most important jargon (memorise these)

| Term / Abbreviation | Plain meaning |
|---|---|
| **SDG** | **Sustainable Development Goal** — 17 global goals set by the United Nations. This form only touches **SDG 4 = "Quality Education."** Codes like `4.3.2` are sub-targets under Goal 4. |
| **ISCED** | International Standard Classification of Education — a worldwide system for ranking education levels. **Post-secondary = ISCED levels 5 to 8** (5=short college, 6=bachelor, 7=master, 8=PhD). |
| **GER** | **Gross Enrolment Ratio** — how many people are enrolled compared to the size of the age group that *could* be enrolled. Shown as a %. |
| **TVET** | Technical and Vocational Education and Training — hands-on, job-skill courses (mechanics, IT, hospitality, etc.). |
| **CVQ** | Caribbean Vocational Qualification — a regional certificate for TVET skills, graded Level 1 to Level 5. |
| **CAPE** | Caribbean Advanced Proficiency Examination — the Caribbean's pre-university exam (like A-Levels). |
| **CPD** | Continuous Professional Development — training/courses a teacher takes to keep their skills current. Measured in **hours in the past 12 months.** |
| **ODA** | Official Development Assistance — foreign aid money (e.g. scholarship funding from another country). |
| **NREN** | National Research and Education Network — a special high-speed internet network for schools/universities. |
| **Parity (M/F)** | Equality between males and females — comparing the male number against the female number. |
| **Attrition** | Teachers **leaving** their jobs (retiring, resigning, transferring). |
| **Trained vs Qualified teacher** | **Trained** = completed a recognised teacher-education course. **Qualified** = meets the national minimum standard for the subject/level. (A form can ask for both.) |
| **Recurrent expenditure** | Regular, repeating running costs (salaries, utilities, supplies) — not one-off purchases. |
| **Personal Emoluments** | A formal word for **staff salaries/pay**. |
| **LDC** | Least Developed Country. |
| **GDP** | Gross Domestic Product — the total size of a country's economy. |

## 3. The 11 sheets, explained one by one

The workbook has 11 tabs. The first four are **instructions/reference**; the rest are **data tables you fill in**.

### Sheet 1 — "SDG Reference" (the key/legend)
A lookup table listing every SDG indicator the form measures. For each one it tells you: the code, a short title, what type of number it is (rate, %, ratio, count, yes/no), the **top of the fraction (numerator)**, the **bottom of the fraction or notes (denominator)**, and which data sheet feeds it. **The indicators covered:**

- **4.3.2** — Tertiary Gross Enrolment Ratio (how many are in college vs the population that could be).
- **4.3.3** — TVET participation rate for ages **15–24** (note: collecting age is *mandatory* for this).
- **4.5.3** — Does a funding system exist to help disadvantaged students? (Yes/No).
- **4.5.4** — How much money is spent per student, by level.
- **4.5.5** — Foreign aid (ODA) to education / scholarship funding source.
- **4.5.6** — Education spending as a % of the country's GDP.
- **4.a.3** — Number of attacks on students/staff/institutions in the year (**zero is a valid answer and must still be reported**).
- **4.b.1** — Scholarship money flows (split: government scholarship / international aid / self-funded).
- **4.c.1** — % of teachers with the minimum required qualifications.
- **4.c.2** — Pupil-to-**trained**-teacher ratio.
- **4.c.3** — % of teachers qualified to national standards, by institution type.
- **4.c.4** — Pupil-to-**qualified**-teacher ratio.
- **4.c.5** — Teacher salary compared to other similar-qualification jobs.
- **4.c.6** — Teacher attrition rate (how many left).
- **4.c.7** — % of teachers who got training (CPD) in the last 12 months.

**Important note on this sheet:** SDG **4.1.1** (minimum reading/math proficiency) is for primary/lower-secondary schools only — **do NOT report post-secondary results under it.** And SDG **4.a.1** (facilities: water, toilets, electricity, internet) is collected on the Finance sheet.

### Sheet 2 — "Cover" (the front page)
Admin details: institution name, who completed it, date, signature, and an "official use only" box for the ministry. Instructions repeat the **15 October 2025** snapshot date. (The "XXXX" placeholders mean the ministry will fill in return address/deadline before sending it out.)

### Sheet 3 — "Definitions" (glossary)
The form's own dictionary. Key definitions: **Age** = age at last birthday; **Dropout** = leaves before finishing for any reason; **Completed Certified** = finished *and* got a certificate; **Completed Uncertified** = finished but got *no* certificate; plus the abbreviations already listed in section 2 above.

### Sheet 4 — "Background" (Section 1 — about the institution)
Basic facts about the institution: address, phone, email, website, date established, head's name, and **management type** (run by Ministry of Education / a Board or Council / Other). Also:
- Staff counts (teaching and non-teaching) and total enrolment.
- Enrolment broken down by **Division** (department) and **gender**.
- Yes/No questions on: strategic plan, **Disaster Management Plan** (SDG 4.a.3), number of emergency drills (4.a.3), disability access (4.a.1), NREN membership (4.a.1), and staff doing academic research.

### Sheet 5 — "Enrolment" (Table T2 — who is studying now)
The big student-numbers table. **One block per Division.** For each programme you record:
- Qualification type (Certificate, CAPE, Associate Degree, Undergraduate, Graduate, Postgraduate, stand-alone courses).
- Whether it's **accredited** (No / Locally / Regionally / Internationally) and whether it's **TVET** (Y/N → feeds 4.3.3).
- Student counts split **by year (1–4), by sex (M/F), and full-time vs part-time.**
- Counts by nationality: **OECS nationals / other CARICOM / other**, each by sex.
- Whether the student is on an **ODA scholarship** (feeds 4.b.1).

*(In the file every data cell is currently 0 — it's a blank template.)*

### Sheet 6 — "Age Dis" (Table T3 — enrolment by age)
Same students, but broken down by **age** (under 16, each year 16–29, then bands 30–34, 35–39, 40+, unknown) and by qualification and sex. **Critical rule:** the **15–24 age rows are mandatory** — they are the only way to calculate the TVET participation rate (SDG 4.3.3). Those rows are marked with arrows pointing to "← SDG 4.3.3."

### Sheet 7 — "Leavers" (Tables T4–T7 — who left and who graduated)
Four sub-tables:
- **T4 — Leavers:** counts of Dropouts, Repeaters, Transfers-out, Completed-Certified, and Completed-Uncertified, by year and sex.
- **T5 — Reasons for dropping out:** tick boxes — Migration, Withdrawal, Expulsion, Financial difficulties, Personal/Family, Other.
- **T6 — Students with disabilities:** count by male/female (feeds equity indicator 4.5.1).
- **T7 — Graduates by field of study:** graduates grouped into the 10 standard ISCED fields (Education; Arts & Humanities; Social Sciences; Business/Law; Natural Sciences/Maths; ICT; Engineering/Manufacturing/Construction; Agriculture; Health & Welfare; Services), by sex.

### Sheet 8 — "Teaching Staff" (Tables T8–T9 — teacher qualifications)
Teacher head-counts by **highest qualification** (PhD, Masters, Postgrad Diploma, Bachelor, Associate, Diploma, Certificate, CAPE/A-Levels) and sex. **You must fill BOTH tables:** T8 = Full-Time staff, T9 = Part-Time staff. Also records totals and **totals of "trained" teachers.** This feeds the teacher-quality and pupil-teacher-ratio indicators (4.c.1–4.c.4).

### Sheet 9 — "Staff Profile" (Table T10 — one row per teacher)
A detailed staff list — **one line per individual teacher** (room for ~50). Columns: name, sex, role/classification (PR=Principal, VP=Vice-Principal, HOD=Head of Department, TT=Trained Teacher), subjects taught, periods taught, years of experience, date of birth, nationality, highest qualification, specialisation, **CPD hours in the past year (required, SDG 4.c.7)**, whether appraised last year, and **whether they left service this year (SDG 4.c.6)**. Row 8 is a filled-in **example** (e.g. "Jane Alexander").

### Sheet 10 — "Exams" (Tables T11–T12 — exam results)
- **T11 — Exam results:** for each division and qualification level (CAPE up to PhD), how many **sat** the exam and how many **passed**, split M/F.
- **T12 — CVQ results:** the same, for vocational CVQ Levels 1–5 (feeds the TVET indicator 4.3.3).

### Sheet 11 — "Finance" (Tables T13–T15 — money and facilities)
- **T13 — Revenue & expenditure (previous year 2024–2025):**
  - *Revenue (money in):* Government, Private, Student Fees, Fundraising, Grants, Sale of services, Consultancy, Other.
  - *Recurrent expenditure (money out):* Staff pay (split teaching/non-teaching), Wages, Utilities, Supplies, Operating & Maintenance, Office, Training, Travel, Other.
- **Equity Funding (SDG 4.5.3):** Yes/No — is there a formal system to redirect resources to disadvantaged students? If yes, describe it and give the total value.
- **Teacher Salary Ratio (SDG 4.c.5):** average teacher salary ÷ average salary in comparable-qualification jobs (calculated automatically).
- **T14 — Water (SDG 4.a.1):** Is safe water available? Are there handwashing facilities with soap? (Yes/No, with a definition of "safe" vs "unsafe" sources.)
- **T15 — Sanitation (SDG 4.a.1):** counts of toilets — staff (M/F/urinals), student (M/F), common-use, and **toilets for students with disabilities.**

## 4. How the pieces fit together (the logic to remember)

1. **It's a once-a-year national statistics return**, snapshot-dated **15 Oct 2025**, for institutions teaching above high-school level.
2. **Every table feeds a UN global goal (SDG 4 = Quality Education).** The whole point of the redesign is that "SDG-tagged" labelling, so the ministry can report internationally without re-processing the data.
3. **Most answers must be split by SEX (M/F)** — gender equality ("parity") is tracked everywhere.
4. **Three things are flagged as especially mandatory / easy to forget:**
   - **Age data for 15–24** (without it, TVET participation can't be calculated).
   - **CPD hours per teacher** (without it, teacher-training indicator fails).
   - **Attacks on students/staff = report even if the answer is zero.**
5. **Numerator ÷ Denominator** thinking runs through it: e.g. enrolment ÷ population = enrolment ratio; trained teachers vs pupils = teacher ratio; teacher pay ÷ other-job pay = salary ratio.
6. In the file itself **all data cells are blank/zero** — it is an empty template to be distributed and completed.
